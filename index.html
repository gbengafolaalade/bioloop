Bioloop has developed a sustainable and cost-effective closed system that produces fish, vegetables, and feed through bio-waste, effectively future proofing Nigeria’s food systems

The Problem

$750bn Food Wastage

30-40% of food produced in Nigeria is wasted. This figure has been estimated higher at about 80% of food wasted in Nigeria, vs 33% in the world

High cost of feed for farmers

Poultry, aquaculture and other livestock operations in Nigeria spend about 70 percent of their operational costs on feed

Uncollected Waste

Large amounts of waste remain uncollected in Nigeria leading to an unsanitary environment

Growing Population

By 2050 Nigeria’s population is expected to be 400 million. Food consumption will continue to grow by 4% per annum creating an urgent need to increase production.

Our Solution

We have designed a facility that adds value to organic waste by leveraging Black Soldier Fly Larvae (BSFL) to produce animal feed and fertilizer for smallholder farmers, reducing the cost of production and boosting efficiency. This facility runs simultaneously with an aquaponics farming system where the fish are fed with BSFL from the previous process. The aquaponics system allows for the production of fish and vegetables at the same time. Our system will allow for food production in urban, high-density areas as well as in areas where access to water is a problem
